## Safety & Regulations

### Non-Negotiable Requirements

1. **Certified Referees Only**
   - All matches must be officiated by certified wrestling referees
   - Referees must be trained in the specific style being wrestled
   - Minimum 1 referee per mat; 2 preferred

2. **Proper Equipment**
   - Regulation wrestling mats (thickness per style requirements)
   - Headgear for all wrestlers (if required by style)
   - Clean, inspected equipment before event

3. **Medical Presence**
   - First aid kit on-site
   - EMT or medical professional present (recommended)
   - Emergency contact info for all participants
   - Venue must have clear emergency exit plan

4. **Participant Requirements**
   - All wrestlers must sign waiver/release form
   - Minors require parent/guardian signature
   - Health screening questionnaire
   - No wrestler competes if injured or ill

### Weight Classes & Age Groups

**Youth (Under 18)**
- Strict weight class matching (¬±2 lbs)
- Age groupings: 6-8, 9-11, 12-14, 15-17
- Shorter match times
- Mandatory protective gear

**Adults (18+)**
- Standard weight classes per wrestling style
- Skill level matching when possible
- Full match duration

**Seniors (50+)**
- Modified rules for safety
- Voluntary participation only
- Emphasis on demonstration, not aggression

### Injury Protocol

1. **Immediate stoppage** if injury occurs
2. **Medical assessment** before resumption
3. **No pressure to continue** ‚Äî safety first always
4. **Incident documentation** for insurance/records

## Cultural Integration

### Why This Matters

The WWWF is not just wrestling ‚Äî it's a **celebration of cultural diversity through wrestling**.

Every local event should:

1. **Honor the local wrestling tradition** (if one exists)
2. **Showcase cultural arts** alongside competition
3. **Educate attendees** about global wrestling styles
4. **Create space** for cultural exchange

### Cultural Programming Ideas

**Opening Ceremony (15-20 min)**
- Local cultural performance (dance, music, storytelling)
- Peace Charter reading in multiple languages
- Symbolic gesture (lighting a flame, raising a flag, etc.)

**Wrestling Demonstration (10-15 min)**
- Showcase different wrestling styles
- Explain rules and cultural significance
- Invite audience participation (kids love this!)

**Intermission Cultural Fair**
- Art installations
- Traditional music performances
- Food from different cultures
- Interactive cultural education booths

**Closing Circle**
- All participants gather
- Share reflections
- Collective commitment to peace
- Singing or chanting (optional, culturally appropriate)

### Working with Cultural Organizations

Partner with:
- **Ethnic community centers**
- **Cultural heritage societies**
- **Immigration/refugee support organizations**
- **International student groups**
- **Interfaith councils**

Ask them:
- What wrestling traditions exist in your culture?
- How can we honor them respectfully?
- Can you contribute a cultural performance?
- Are there community members who would like to participate?

## Livestream Setup

### Why Livestream?

Livestreaming connects your local event to the **global WWWF network**, allowing:

- Real-time sharing across time zones
- "Follow the Earth" viewer experience
- Archival documentation
- Extended community participation

### Basic Setup

**Minimum Requirements:**
- Smartphone with camera
- Stable internet connection (4G or WiFi)
- Free streaming platform (YouTube Live, Twitch, Facebook Live)

**Procedure:**
1. Create streaming account
2. Test connection before event
3. Submit stream URL to WWWF platform
4. Go live 15 min before start time
5. Keep stream active for full event

### Intermediate Setup

**Equipment:**
- Camera (DSLR, camcorder, or quality webcam)
- Tripod or stable mounting
- External microphone (for better audio)
- Laptop with OBS or streaming software
- Ethernet connection (preferred over WiFi)

**Quality Tips:**
- Good lighting on wrestling mat
- Audio mix: Ref calls audible, music not overpowering
- Multiple camera angles (if possible)
- Lower thirds with wrestler names, match info

### Advanced Setup

**Multi-camera production with:**
- Switcher for live camera cuts
- Dedicated audio mixer
- Graphics overlay (scores, timer)
- Commentary desk (optional)
- Social media integration (live chat, Q&A)

**Submit to WWWF:**
- High-quality stream URL
- Backup stream URL
- Social media handles for cross-promotion

### Content Guidelines

‚úÖ **Do Stream:**
- All matches and competitions
- Cultural performances
- Peace Charter reading
- Opening/closing ceremonies
- Crowd reactions, atmosphere

‚ùå **Don't Stream:**
- Private conversations
- Minors without parental consent (get waivers!)
- Medical incidents (pause stream)
- Inappropriate behavior (cut away immediately)

## Local Chapter Formation

### What is a Local Chapter?

A **WWWF Local Chapter** is a self-organizing group committed to:

1. Hosting annual WWWF events in their city
2. Upholding the Peace Charter
3. Coordinating with the global network
4. Maintaining transparency and grassroots values

### How to Form a Chapter

**Step 1: Assemble Core Team**
- Minimum 3 people
- Diverse skills: event planning, wrestling knowledge, community outreach
- Shared commitment to WWWF values

**Step 2: Register Chapter**
- Visit [WWWF Platform] and register your city
- Agree to Peace Charter
- Provide contact info and leadership structure

**Step 3: Establish Governance**
- How will decisions be made?
- Who are the points of contact?
- How will funds be managed transparently?
- What are conflict resolution protocols?

**Step 4: Connect to Network**
- Join global coordination calls (monthly)
- Share your event plans
- Collaborate with other chapters
- Contribute ideas to platform development

### Chapter Responsibilities

- ‚úÖ Host at least one WWWF event annually
- ‚úÖ Uphold Peace Charter in all activities
- ‚úÖ Submit event data (attendance, impact) to global network
- ‚úÖ Maintain financial transparency
- ‚úÖ Engage with community year-round, not just during festival

### Chapter Autonomy

You have freedom to:
- Set your own event format (within safety guidelines)
- Showcase your local culture
- Partner with local organizations
- Raise funds independently
- Adapt WWWF to your community's needs

You must:
- Maintain nonviolence commitment
- Avoid political co-opting
- Stay grassroots and citizen-led
- Share learnings with global network

## Sponsorship & Funding

### Funding Models

**1. Grassroots / Volunteer-Run**
- No admission fee
- Donated venue and equipment
- Volunteer staff
- In-kind contributions (food, printing, etc.)

**2. Community-Supported**
- Small admission fee ($5-20)
- Local business sponsorships
- Crowdfunding campaign
- Merchandise sales (posters, t-shirts)

**3. Grant-Funded**
- Apply for peace/arts/sports grants
- Partner with NGOs
- Cultural institution support
- Educational institution collaboration

### Acceptable Sponsors

‚úÖ **Allowed:**
- Local businesses (restaurants, gyms, etc.)
- Sports equipment companies
- Arts organizations
- Cultural institutions
- Educational organizations
- Peace-focused NGOs

‚ùå **Not Allowed:**
- Weapons manufacturers
- Alcohol/tobacco companies (for youth events)
- Politically partisan organizations
- Companies with human rights violations
- Entities that conflict with Peace Charter values

### Financial Transparency

All chapters must:

1. **Document all funding sources** publicly
2. **Publish budget** before and after event
3. **Share financial report** with global coordination council
4. **Ensure no personal profit** for organizers (reimbursement OK, payment not)

## Day-of-Event Logistics

### Recommended Event Flow

#### 8:00 AM ‚Äî Setup
- Venue opens
- Mat installation
- Sound/tech setup
- Signage and decorations
- Volunteer briefing

#### 10:00 AM ‚Äî Registration Opens
- Participant check-in
- Weigh-ins (if required)
- Waivers signed
- Event program distribution

#### 12:00 PM ‚Äî Event Begins

**12:00 - 12:15 PM: Opening Cultural Ceremony**
- Welcome by organizers
- Local cultural performance
- Purpose of WWWF explained

**12:15 - 12:25 PM: Peace Charter Reading**
- Read aloud in multiple languages
- Invite attendees to sign (physical or digital)
- Moment of silence for peace

**12:30 - 1:30 PM: Youth Wrestling Matches**
- Kids and teens compete
- Short, energetic, fun
- Crowd engagement high

**1:30 - 2:00 PM: Cultural Style Showcase**
- Demonstration of different wrestling styles
- Educational component
- Invite volunteers from audience

**2:00 - 2:30 PM: Music & Art Intermission**
- Live music performance
- Food and refreshments
- Art viewing
- Social mingling

**2:30 - 4:00 PM: Main Exhibition Matches**
- Adult competitions
- Highlight local talent
- Respect and sportsmanship emphasized

**4:00 - 4:05 PM: Global Synchronization Moment**
- Pause for global anthem/silence
- Connect with WWWF events worldwide
- Powerful symbolic unity

**4:05 - 4:30 PM: Final Matches**
- Closing competitions
- Awards/recognition (optional)

**4:30 - 4:45 PM: Closing Communal Circle**
- All participants gather
- Reflections shared
- Commitment to peace reaffirmed
- Thank you to all involved

**4:45 - 5:00 PM: Closing Performance**
- Musical sendoff
- Celebratory atmosphere
- Invitation to next event

#### 5:00 PM ‚Äî Teardown
- Mat breakdown
- Equipment packed
- Venue cleaned
- Volunteer thank-you

### Volunteer Roles

- **Event Coordinator** (1): Oversees everything
- **Registration Desk** (2-3): Check-in, waivers
- **Referee Coordinator** (1): Manages officials
- **Music/Arts Coordinator** (1): Cues performances
- **Tech/Livestream Operator** (1-2): Manages stream
- **Hospitality** (2-3): Food, water, comfort
- **Photographer/Videographer** (1-2): Documentation
- **Social Media** (1): Live posting, engagement
- **Safety/First Aid** (1): Medical response
- **Greeters** (2-4): Welcome, guide attendees

### Emergency Protocols

**Medical Emergency:**
1. Stop all activities immediately
2. Administer first aid
3. Call 911 if serious
4. Document incident
5. Notify participants' emergency contacts

**Weather Emergency (outdoor events):**
1. Monitor forecast closely
2. Have indoor backup plan
3. Communicate changes clearly
4. Prioritize safety over schedule

**Disruption/Conflict:**
1. Remain calm
2. De-escalate verbally
3. Remove disruptive individual if necessary
4. Law enforcement if needed (rare)
5. Document incident
